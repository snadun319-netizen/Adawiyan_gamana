Waterfalls of Sri Lanka

Sri Lanka is home to some of the world's most beautiful waterfalls. From the towering Bambarakanda Falls to the mythical Ravana Falls, each cascade offers unique beauty and adventure.

Location Map

Major waterfalls are located throughout Sri Lanka's central highlands and southern regions

Moderate

Bambarakanda Falls

263 meters (863 feet)
Kalupahana

The tallest waterfall in Sri Lanka, Bambarakanda Falls cascades down a mountainside in the central highlands. The waterfall is fed by the Kuda Oya stream and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding tea plantations.

Best time: December to March
Highlights:
  • Tallest waterfall in Sri Lanka
  • Scenic hiking trails
  • Tea plantation views

Difficult

Diyaluma Falls

220 meters (720 feet)
Koslanda

The second highest waterfall in Sri Lanka, Diyaluma Falls features natural infinity pools at the top with stunning panoramic views. The waterfall is accessible via a challenging hike through beautiful countryside.

Best time: January to April
Highlights:
  • Natural infinity pools
  • Panoramic mountain views
  • Swimming opportunities
View Details
Easy

Ravana Falls

25 meters (82 feet)
Ella

One of the widest waterfalls in Sri Lanka, Ravana Falls is steeped in legend from the Ramayana. Located along the main road to Ella, it's easily accessible and offers a refreshing bathing spot.

Best time: May to September
Highlights:
  • Mythological significance
  • Easy accessibility
  • Swimming pool at base

Moderate

Dunhinda Falls

64 meters (210 feet)
Badulla

Named for the smoky dew drops that form a mist at the foot of the falls, Dunhinda is one of Sri Lanka's most beautiful waterfalls. The name means "mist-laden" in Sinhala.

Best time: November to March
Highlights:
  • Misty atmosphere
  • Beautiful hiking trail
  • Dense forest surroundings

Ramboda Falls

109 meters (358 feet)
Ramboda

Located along the Colombo-Badulla road, Ramboda Falls consists of three sections. The waterfall is surrounded by lush tea estates and offers spectacular views.

Best time: December to April
Highlights:
  • Three-tiered waterfall
  • Tea estate views
  • Roadside accessibility

Devon Falls

97 meters (318 feet)
Talawakele

Named after a pioneer English coffee planter, Devon Falls is visible from the main road and cascades through verdant tea plantations in the central highlands.

Best time: Year-round
Highlights:
  • Colonial history
  • Tea plantation backdrop
  • Viewpoint access

Visiting Tips

Best Time to Visit

The best time to see waterfalls at full flow is during the monsoon seasons. South-west monsoon (May-September) for western areas, and north-east monsoon (December-February) for eastern regions.

Safety First

Always stay on marked paths, wear appropriate footwear with good grip, avoid swimming in unknown areas, and never attempt to climb wet rocks near waterfalls.

What to Bring

Pack water, snacks, waterproof bags for electronics, swimwear if swimming is allowed, insect repellent, and a good camera to capture the beauty.
